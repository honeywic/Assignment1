About Me

My name is Bahati George James.
I am a first-year student at the University of Dar es Salaam,currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science.
I chose this field because I believe that technology, when guided by people who understand people, has the power to change lives
and I want to be one of those people who bridges that gap between code and community.

My academic journey began at Riverside Primary School, where curiosity became a habit.
I went on to Canossa High School for my Ordinary Level studies, then to Cornerstone Leadership Academy for my Advanced Level,
where I studied Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.
Each institution gave me something that stayed with me: discipline, critical thinking, and the confidence to lead.
Now at UDSM, I am combining all of that with the technical skills of computer science.

Hobbies & Interests

  • Reading

    Books are very interesting things as they not only make you enjoy,
    but they also make you better and teach you to become more efficient.
    I am drawn especially to books about human behaviour, communication, and self-development.
    Dale Carnegie is one of the authors that I really appreciate.
    His books like How to Start Public Speaking gave me the courage to own a room,
    and How to Win Friends and Influence People completely changed how I listen to and connect with others.
    I started reading at high school inspired by my mentor Tim Krutter who says A good leader is a good reader.

  • Singing

    Music is something that I love.
    I used to sing in a choir and at high school I had a privilege of serving as a Choirmistress.
    My work was leading rehearsals and helping to organize harmonies in a choir.
    I currently still practise singing by listening to various choirs and harmonising with them.

  • Debating

    Debating is the place where reading, communication, and pressure all meet.
    I have competed at the Tanzania National Debates Championship
    and the East African Debates Championship, two experiences that tested my ability to think fast,
    argue clearly, and engage respectfully with people who completely disagreed with me.
    Debating taught me that being persuasive is not about being the loudest voice in the room.
    It is about being the clearest one.
